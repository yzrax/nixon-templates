A firm built
on principle

Pearson Hardmann was founded in 1948 by Arthur Pearson and James Hardmann, two attorneys who believed the law should be practiced with both rigor and humanity. That belief still guides every file we open and every client we represent.

We are a boutique firm practicing in commercial litigation, corporate law, estate planning, and civil defense. Our size is deliberate — we remain small enough that every client works directly with a partner, yet deep enough in experience to handle matters of substantial complexity.

Over seventy-five years, the firm has represented individuals, family businesses, and Fortune 500 companies. What unites these engagements is not their scale but the standard we apply to each: thorough preparation, honest communication, and unwavering advocacy.

We do not take every case that comes through the door. When we accept a matter, it is because we believe in the merits and are confident we can make a difference.

Areas of
practice

Matters we handle with depth, discretion, and a track record of results.

Complex business disputes, contract litigation, shareholder conflicts, and regulatory defense. We represent plaintiffs and defendants in state and federal courts, arbitrations, and mediations. Our approach combines aggressive preparation with strategic judgment about when to litigate and when to resolve.

Entity formation, governance, mergers and acquisitions, securities compliance, and general corporate counsel. We advise startups through established enterprises, providing practical guidance that balances legal risk with business objectives.

Trusts, wills, estate administration, tax strategy, and family wealth transfer. We work closely with clients and their financial advisors to build plans that protect assets, respect family dynamics, and ensure your intentions are honored.

Professional liability, personal injury defense, product liability, and insurance coverage disputes. We defend individuals, businesses, and insurers with meticulous case development and a focus on achieving favorable outcomes before and during trial.
